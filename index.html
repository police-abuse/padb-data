Police Abuse Database

The police abuse database attempts to collect statistics about clear cases of police abusing their position in ways that violate the rights of the public. The information is sourced from publicly-disclosed articles that are reported in the news. The database is limited to instances of police abuse in the United States. If youre interested in auditing the findings or doing your own analysis of the incidents you can download the raw data here: https://github.com/police-abuse/padb-data

Database Composition

At this time, the database is made up of 151 incidents collected from public articles.

What kind of abuse are we talking?

The database collects information on six broad types of abuse with sub-varieties under each.

  • Abuse of force: using a weapon or inflicting pain on someone when it is not necessary
  • Improper search: searching a person without cause or warrant
  • General misconduct: includes falsifying reports, theft, testilying, and false arrest.
  • Intimidation: verbal abuse, threats, and excessive show of force (for example, unnecessary SWAT team deployment)
  • Sexual conduct: Anything sexual.

That looks like it adds up to more than 151 incidents! Yes is probably does. The reason is because there are relatively few incidents where there is only one type of abuse. When an officer beats someone up (Abuse of force) he or she almost always arrests the person for resisting or disorderly conduct (General misconduct).
